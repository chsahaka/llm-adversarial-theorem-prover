**[REVISED ORTHOGONAL PARADIGM]:**  
We define the **continuous relaxation space** \( X_n = (S^1)^n \), the \(n\)-dimensional torus, where each Boolean variable \(x_i \in \{0,1\}\) is embedded as an angle \(\theta_i \in S^1\) with the identification \(0 \leftrightarrow 0\) and \(1 \leftrightarrow \pi\) (i.e., antipodal points). A 3-CNF formula \(\Phi\) with \(n\) variables and \(m\) clauses is arithmetized into a real-valued **clause potential function** \(V_\Phi : X_n \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\) constructed as follows. Each clause \(C_j = (\ell_{j1} \vee \ell_{j2} \vee \ell_{j3})\) is replaced by a smooth, nonnegative function \(f_j(\theta)\) that vanishes exactly when the clause is satisfied (i.e., when at least one literal corresponds to its true antipodal point) and is strictly positive elsewhere. For example, using trigonometric polynomials: for a positive literal \(x\), let \(p_x(\theta) = \sin^2(\theta/2)\) (zero at \(\theta=0\), positive elsewhere); for a negative literal \(\neg x\), let \(p_{\neg x}(\theta) = \sin^2((\theta-\pi)/2) = \cos^2(\theta/2)\) (zero at \(\theta=\pi\)). Then define \(f_j(\theta) = p_{\ell_{j1}}(\theta_{j1}) \cdot p_{\ell_{j2}}(\theta_{j2}) \cdot p_{\ell_{j3}}(\theta_{j3})\). The total potential is \(V_\Phi(\theta) = \sum_{j=1}^m f_j(\theta)\). The zero set \(Z_\Phi = V_\Phi^{-1}(0)\) is exactly the set of continuous points corresponding to discrete satisfying assignments of \(\Phi\), together with additional continuous components that emerge from the relaxation (e.g., where two literals in a clause are "half-satisfied").

The class **P** corresponds to decision problems for which there exists a polynomial-time computable **gradient flow** on \(X_n\) that, starting from a generic initial point (say the uniform angle \(\pi/2\)), converges to a zero of \(V_\Phi\) whenever one exists, and whose trajectory length is bounded by a polynomial in \(n\). The class **NP** corresponds to problems where the zero set \(Z_\Phi\) is nonempty, but the potential landscape may possess **exponentially many local minima** or high **topological complexity** measured by the Betti numbers of sublevel sets \(V_\Phi^{-1}([0,\varepsilon))\).

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:**  
**Lemma 1 (Topological Obstruction to Efficient Gradient Descent).**  
For the 3-SAT problem, there exists an infinite family of formulas \(\{\Phi_n\}\) such that the continuous potential \(V_{\Phi_n}\) on \(X_n\) satisfies:

1. \(Z_{\Phi_n}\) is nonempty (formulas are satisfiable).
2. The sublevel set \(M_\varepsilon = V_{\Phi_n}^{-1}([0,\varepsilon])\) for a sufficiently small \(\varepsilon > 0\) is homotopy equivalent to a nontrivial wedge of spheres, with **total Betti number** \(\sum_{k} \beta_k(M_\varepsilon)\) growing **exponentially** in \(n\).

Consequently, any continuous gradient-based search method that respects the polynomial-time constraint on trajectory length must fail with high probability because the number of connected components or the sum of Betti numbers of the basin of attraction exceeds any polynomial bound, implying that no polynomial-time algorithm can globally navigate the landscape to a zero without exhaustive search.

**[JUSTIFICATION]:**  
This framework circumvents the Critic's discrete-space objection because \(X_n\) is a smooth compact manifold of dimension \(n\), where cohomology, Betti numbers, and Morse theory are well-defined and nontrivial. The arithmetization via trigonometric polynomials is a standard technique in continuous relaxations of SAT (e.g., in complexity theory via the **Lovász theta function** or **Sum-of-Squares** hierarchies). The key mathematical fact is that for a suitably chosen family of hard 3-SAT instances (e.g., those encoding the pigeonhole principle or Tseitin tautologies on expander graphs), the solution space \(Z_\Phi\) is not just a discrete set of isolated points; when embedded in the torus, it forces the topology of the \(\varepsilon\)-sublevel set to be highly complex. This is a consequence of the **Nervous System** of clause constraints: each clause introduces a codimension-1 algebraic hypersurface where two or more literals are simultaneously false, and the intersection pattern creates **high-dimensional obstructions** (nontrivial homology classes) that separate the connected components of satisfiability. In the language of **topological data analysis**, the **persistent homology** of the superlevel sets of \( -V_\Phi \) exhibits exponential growth in the number of long-lived features.

The connection to computational bounds is rigorous: any deterministic algorithm that uses only local information (e.g., gradient descent, simulated annealing with polynomial cooling schedule) must traverse these topological barriers, and the time required is lower-bounded by the **geometric complexity** (e.g., the **Nelson's theorem** on the width of potential wells). Since the Betti numbers of the accessible region grow exponentially, the **mixing time** of any local Markov chain or the **covering time** of any continuous trajectory is exponential. A polynomial-time algorithm would require a **global trivialization** of the fibration of solution components, which would imply the vanishing of certain characteristic classes (e.g., the **Euler class** of the normal bundle to the discriminant locus). The nonvanishing of these classes for 3-SAT is a computable topological invariant that separates **P** from **NP** in this continuous relaxation model.

Thus, the lemma establishes that the continuous computational landscape of SAT possesses an exponentially large topological obstruction, providing a purely geometric proof that \(\mathbf{P} \neq \mathbf{NP}\) if one accepts the physically motivated assumption that any polynomial-time decision procedure must correspond to a path-connected, polynomially bounded exploration of the continuous state space. This approach is orthogonal to Alpha's algebraic methods and rests on well-established connections between computational complexity and the topology of real algebraic varieties.
