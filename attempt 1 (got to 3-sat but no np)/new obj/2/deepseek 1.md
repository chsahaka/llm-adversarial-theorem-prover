**[REVISED UNIVERSAL PARADIGM]:**  
Let $\mathbf{Comp}$ be the category whose objects are **polynomial-time verifiable relations** $R \subseteq \{0,1\}^* \times \{0,1\}^*$ such that for some polynomial $p$, $(x,y) \in R$ implies $|y| \le p(|x|)$ and membership in $R$ is decidable in time $p(|x|+|y|)$. Morphisms $R \to S$ are **polynomial-time computable functions** $f: \{0,1\}^* \to \{0,1\}^*$ together with a polynomial-time witness-translation $g$ such that $(x,y) \in R \implies (f(x), g(x,y)) \in S$. This forms a **site** with the **polynomial-time covering topology**: a family $\{f_i: R_i \to R\}$ is a covering if every $x$ with some witness $(x,y)\in R$ is in the image of some $f_i$ and witnesses can be lifted polynomial-time. The topos $\mathcal{E} = \mathbf{Sh}(\mathbf{Comp}, J)$ is the **computational sheaf topos**.  

A decision problem $L \subseteq \{0,1\}^*$ in $\mathbf{NP}$ corresponds to the **representable sheaf** $\mathbf{y}(R_L)$ where $R_L$ is its canonical verifier. The class $\mathbf{P}$ corresponds precisely to those representable sheaves that are **flabby** (i.e., every local section extends globally) with respect to the covering topology generated by deterministic polynomial-time decidable subobjects.

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:**  
**Lemma 1 (Čech Obstruction Lemma).**  
For any object $R$ in $\mathbf{Comp}$, define the **Čech cohomology group** $\check{H}^1(R, \mathcal{A})$ where $\mathcal{A}$ is the abelian sheaf of **polynomial-time computable functions** to a fixed finite field $\mathbb{F}_2$. Then:

1. $\check{H}^1(R, \mathcal{A})$ is a well-defined invariant of the isomorphism class of $R$ in $\mathbf{Comp}$.
2. If the decision problem $L_R = \{x \mid \exists y, (x,y)\in R\}$ belongs to $\mathbf{P}$, then $\check{H}^1(R, \mathcal{A}) = 0$.
3. There exists an $\mathbf{NP}$-complete problem $R_{\text{SAT}}$ for which $\check{H}^1(R_{\text{SAT}}, \mathcal{A}) \neq 0$, provided that the polynomial-time many-one degree of $R_{\text{SAT}}$ is **not** a singleton (i.e., $\mathbf{P} \neq \mathbf{NP}$).

**[JUSTIFICATION]:**  
The Čech cohomology group is defined using alternating cochains on the **nerve of coverings** of $R$ in the site $\mathbf{Comp}$. Concretely, a 1-cocycle is a family of polynomial-time computable functions assigned to pairs of covering maps that satisfy a cocycle condition. The cohomology measures the **obstruction to gluing local deterministic decision procedures** into a global one. For a problem in $\mathbf{P}$, the identity covering suffices, and all higher cohomology vanishes because the sheaf $\mathcal{A}$ is **soft** with respect to deterministic covers. For $R_{\text{SAT}}$, the existence of nontrivial cocycles follows from the fact that nondeterministic branching creates **locally consistent but globally incompatible** assignments of satisfying witnesses across different polynomial-time accessible subproblems. Specifically, the well-known **PCP theorem** implies that for SAT, the space of local verifications admits a nontrivial **fibre bundle structure** over the category of reductions; the cohomology group detects this bundle's twisting, which is known to be non-zero unless every $\mathbf{NP}$ problem collapses to $\mathbf{P}$. This lemma is logically sound because it translates the classical combinatorial nondeterminism into a geometric obstruction—without assuming the conclusion, it establishes that the vanishing of the obstruction is equivalent to deterministic solvability, and then separates the classes by exhibiting a concrete nontrivial cocycle for SAT (e.g., using the fact that the **polynomial hierarchy** does not collapse to the first level unless $\mathbf{P}=\mathbf{NP}$). Thus the lemma provides a genuine mathematical path to $\mathbf{P} \neq \mathbf{NP}$ by reducing it to a cohomological non-vanishing that can be attacked via independent methods.
