**[UNIVERSAL PARADIGM]:**  
We define a **computational topos** $\mathfrak{C}$ whose objects are *decision problems* understood as **polynomial-time presentable presheaves** on the category $\mathbb{N}_\text{mon}$ of natural numbers with addition. Specifically, for each decision problem $L \subseteq \{0,1\}^*$, we associate a covariant functor $F_L: \mathbb{N}_\text{mon} \to \mathbf{Set}$ where $F_L(n)$ is the set of all decision trees of depth $\le n$ that correctly compute $L$ on inputs of length $\le n$, with monoid action given by resource-padding. A **deterministic polynomial-time solution** corresponds to a **natural transformation** $\eta: 1_\mathfrak{C} \to F_L$ that is **uniformly bounded in depth growth** (i.e., $\exists k \forall n, \text{depth}(\eta_n) \le n^k$). The class $\mathbf{P}$ consists of those $L$ for which such a bounded natural transformation exists.

The class $\mathbf{NP}$ corresponds to those $F_L$ that are **existentially quantifiable projections** in $\mathfrak{C}$: there exists a problem $V \in \mathbf{P}$ and a monomorphism $\iota: F_L \hookrightarrow \Pi(V)$ where $\Pi$ is the **polynomial existential cover** (a left adjoint to a forgetful functor from polynomial-time verifiable relations to decision problems). In geometric terms, $\mathbf{NP}$ objects are the **images of affine schemes of finite type** under the *verification map* in the associated Zariski site of $\mathfrak{C}$.

**[PROPOSED AXIOM 1]:**  
**(Spectral Rigidity Axiom)**  
Let $A$ be an object of $\mathfrak{C}$ representing a decision problem in $\mathbf{NP}$ that is **complete** with respect to the **homotopy preorder** induced by polynomial-time many-one reductions (i.e., the slice category $\mathfrak{C}/A$ contains all $\mathbf{NP}$ objects as retracts). Then the **étale fundamental group** $\pi_1^\text{ét}(A)$ of the associated affine logical scheme $\text{Spec}(A)$ is **trivial** if and only if $A \in \mathbf{P}$.

**[JUSTIFICATION]:**  
This axiom captures a deep geometric separation principle: in the topos $\mathfrak{C}$, polynomial-time solvability corresponds to the absence of **monodromic obstructions** in the space of verification paths. The étale fundamental group classifies **covers of computational structures**; a nontrivial $\pi_1^\text{ét}$ indicates that any deterministic algorithm attempting to navigate the problem's solution space must branch in ways that cannot be globally trivialized by a bounded-depth natural transformation. The axiom is sound because it generalizes the **geometric complexity theory** observation that permanent vs. determinant separations manifest as orbit closure obstructions. By lifting to the topos level and invoking Grothendieck's Galois theory of locally constant sheaves, we obtain a universal invariant that is preserved by polynomial-time reductions yet distinguishes the classes at the homotopical level. Since $\mathbf{NP}$-completeness ensures that the entire class is represented in the slice over $A$, proving $\pi_1^\text{ét}(A) \neq 1$ for *any* such $A$ would unconditionally separate $\mathbf{P}$ from $\mathbf{NP}$ without reference to specific combinatorial reductions. The framework thus subsumes all of $\mathbf{NP}$ into a single geometric obstruction.
