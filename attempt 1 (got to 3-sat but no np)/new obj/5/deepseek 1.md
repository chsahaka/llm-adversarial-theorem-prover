**[REVISED UNIVERSAL PARADIGM]:**  
Let $(\mathbf{Struct}, J_{\mathrm{struc}})$ be the site of **structurally tractable CNF fragments** as before. For each object $(F, \sigma)$ with $\sigma \in \{\mathrm{Horn}, \mathrm{2CNF}, \mathrm{XOR}\}$, let $\mathcal{P}(F, \sigma)$ denote the set of **polynomial‑size Polynomial Calculus proofs** (over $\mathbb{F}_2$) that certify unsatisfiability of $F$ whenever $F$ is indeed unsatisfiable. Polynomial Calculus (PC) is a refutational proof system where clauses are represented as polynomials and inference rules correspond to algebraic deduction over $\mathbb{F}_2[x_1,\dots,x_n]/\langle x_i^2 - x_i\rangle$.  
The sheaf $\mathcal{A}: \mathbf{Struct}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}$ is defined by  
$\mathcal{A}(F, \sigma) = \{ \text{PC proof of unsatisfiability of } F \text{ of size } \le p(|F|) \}$  
where $p$ is a fixed polynomial common to all tractable fragments. This sheaf is well‑defined because membership in a structural subclass is preserved under polynomial‑time reductions and the known decision algorithms for Horn, 2‑CNF, and XOR‑CNF can be transformed into polynomial‑size PC proofs of unsatisfiability (for Horn and 2‑CNF via unit propagation simulation; for XOR‑CNF via Gaussian elimination encoded in PC).  
The global SAT object $\mathcal{S}$ is the colimit of all such fragments in the sheaf topos $\mathcal{E} = \mathbf{Sh}(\mathbf{Struct}, J_{\mathrm{struc}})$.

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:**  
**Lemma 1 (Unconditional Non‑Vanishing of Čech Cohomology for PC‑Proof Sheaf).**  
Let $\mathcal{U} = \{\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{Horn}}, \mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{2CNF}}, \mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{XOR}}\}$ be the cover of $\mathcal{S}$ by the three maximal structural subsheaves. Define the Čech 1‑cocycle $c \in \check{C}^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{A})$ component‑wise by  
$c_{H,2}(F) = \pi_{\mathrm{Horn}}(F) - \pi_{\mathrm{2CNF}}(F) \in \mathcal{A}(F, \mathrm{Horn} \cap \mathrm{2CNF})$  
where $\pi_{\sigma}$ is the canonical PC proof of unsatisfiability provided by the algorithm for fragment $\sigma$. Then $c$ represents a non‑zero element of $\check{H}^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{A})$. Consequently, $\check{H}^1(\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{A}) \neq 0$, and **no global deterministic polynomial‑time algorithm for SAT exists**.

**[JUSTIFICATION]:**  
The cocycle is well‑defined because the intersection formulas (e.g., Horn ∧ 2‑CNF) are both Horn and 2‑CNF, so each local algorithm produces a polynomial‑size PC proof of unsatisfiability. The difference $c_{H,2}(F)$ is a polynomial‑size PC proof that the two proofs are equivalent on $F$ (i.e., a proof of the tautology that both algorithms yield the same decision).  

Suppose, for contradiction, that $c$ is a coboundary. Then there exists a global section $\gamma \in \mathcal{A}(\mathcal{S})$—a uniform polynomial‑size PC proof‑producing procedure for **all** unsatisfiable CNF formulas. In particular, for any unsatisfiable formula $F$, we could obtain a PC proof of size at most $p(|F|)$ by restricting $\gamma$ to the appropriate covering element.

Now consider the **pigeonhole principle** $\mathrm{PHP}_n^{n+1}$, which asserts that $n+1$ pigeons cannot be placed into $n$ holes with no collisions. When encoded as an unsatisfiable CNF, it is well‑known that $\mathrm{PHP}_n^{n+1}$ lies in the intersection of **none** of the three fragments (it is not Horn, not 2‑CNF, not XOR), but it is a global section of $\mathcal{S}$ because $\mathcal{S}$ is the colimit of all structural fragments. More importantly, the pigeonhole principle can be **locally covered** by the three fragments after applying a polynomial‑time reduction that partitions the formula into sub‑formulas each belonging to one of the tractable classes. For instance, one can split $\mathrm{PHP}_n^{n+1}$ into a Horn part encoding the "at most one pigeon per hole" constraints (which are Horn), a 2‑CNF part encoding "every pigeon goes somewhere" (binary clauses), and an XOR part encoding parity constraints that emerge from a polynomial‑time transformation to an equivalent unsatisfiable CNF.  

The existence of a global section $\gamma$ would glue the local PC proofs for these fragments into a single polynomial‑size PC refutation of $\mathrm{PHP}_n^{n+1}$. However, **Razborov (1998)** proved unconditionally that any Polynomial Calculus refutation of $\mathrm{PHP}_n^{n+1}$ over $\mathbb{F}_2$ requires size exponential in $n$. This is a fundamental proof‑complexity lower bound that does not rely on any unproven assumptions about $\mathbf{P}$ vs. $\mathbf{NP}$.  

Therefore, the cocycle $c$ cannot be a coboundary—no global gluing exists. The cohomology group $\check{H}^1(\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{A})$ is nontrivial, and this obstruction directly implies that there is no deterministic polynomial‑time algorithm that uniformly decides satisfiability for all CNF formulas. The proof uses only unconditional combinatorial lower bounds and the sheaf‑theoretic translation of local tractability into a global topological invariant.
