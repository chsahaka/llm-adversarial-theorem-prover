### The Problem Disguise: "The Diplomatic Banquet"
We are going to use a reskinned version of the **3-SAT problem** (the foundational NP-complete problem). We will ask the AI to prove whether a fast, universally scaling algorithm exists to solve it, or if one is mathematically impossible. 

Here are the exact prompts to initialize your three AIs.

---

### AI Window 1: The Prover (DeepSeek - DeepThink)
**Purpose:** This is your engine. DeepSeek will attempt to construct the proof. We force it to output only one small step at a time to prevent it from hallucinating a massive, fake 10-page proof.

**Initial Prompt (Copy and paste this):**
> "You are an elite, hyper-rational logician. We are analyzing a theoretical puzzle called the 'Diplomatic Banquet Protocol'. 
>
> **The Rules:**
> 1. You have a massive banquet with $N$ delegates. 
> 2. You are given a list of 'Demands'. Each demand is a group of exactly 3 specific delegates. 
> 3. For every single demand on the list, **at least one** of those 3 delegates must be seated at the Main Table. 
> 4. Delegates can either be at the Main Table or the Side Table (binary state).
> 5. We know that if someone hands us a seating chart, it is extremely fast to verify if it satisfies all demands (time taken scales predictably with the number of demands).
>
> **The Objective:**
> We need to know if it is logically possible to create a universal, deterministic set of instructions (an algorithm) that will *always* find a valid seating chart (or prove none exists) in a number of steps bounded by a polynomial function of $N$ ($N^k$). 
>
> Your task is to mathematically prove either that such a fast instruction set CAN exist, or definitively prove that it CANNOT exist. 
>
> **Constraints for your output:**
> Do NOT attempt to solve this all at once. We are taking this one micro-step at a time. 
> For your first output, define the mathematical framework and variables we will use, and propose exactly ONE foundational lemma or logical starting point to begin your proof. Format your response strictly as:
> **[FRAMEWORK]:** (Your variables and definitions)
> **[PROPOSED STEP 1]:** (Your first logical deduction or lemma)
> **[JUSTIFICATION]:** (Why this step is mathematically sound)"

---

### AI Window 2: The Ruthless Critic (Gemini 3.1 Pro - #1)
**Purpose:** This AI prevents DeepSeek from hallucinating. It acts as an unforgiving mathematical peer reviewer. 

**Initial Prompt (Copy and paste this):**
> "You are a ruthless, pedantic, and unforgiving mathematical reviewer. Your sole purpose is to find hidden assumptions, logical leaps, edge cases, and fallacies in logical arguments. 
>
> We are analyzing the 'Diplomatic Banquet Protocol', where we must assign $N$ delegates to either a Main Table or Side Table. We are given a list of 'Demands' (groups of 3 delegates), and for every demand, at least one delegate must be at the Main Table. We are trying to prove if a deterministic method exists to solve this in polynomial time ($N^k$).
>
> I will provide you with a proposed logical step from a colleague. You must attack it. 
> Do not be polite. If there is even a 1% chance the logic does not hold for a massive value of $N$, or if it relies on an unproven heuristic, you must fail it. 
>
> **Respond strictly in this format:**
> **[VERDICT]:** (Write either 'AIRTIGHT' or 'FLAW FOUND')
> **[THE FLAW]:** (If flawed, explain exactly where the logic breaks down or what assumption was made).
> **[QUESTION FOR PROVER]:** (Ask one piercing question that forces the prover to confront the flaw).
> 
> Acknowledge you understand your role by saying 'I am ready to review Step 1'."

### How to run the loop (Your role as the Human API):

Once all three are initialized, you become the messenger. 
1. Read **DeepSeek's** response (Step 1).
2. Copy DeepSeek's response and paste it to the **Critic (Gemini 1)**.
3. Take the Critic's response AND DeepSeek's response, and paste them both into the **Tracker (Gemini 2)**. 
   *(Format: "Here is Prover's Step: [Paste]. Here is Critic's Verdict: [Paste].")*
4. Take the **[NEXT PROMPT FOR PROVER]** generated by the Tracker, and paste it back into **DeepSeek**.
5. Repeat.

### What to expect:
For the first few steps, DeepSeek will do a great job setting up the formal logic. It will correctly translate the problem into boolean algebra or graph theory (even without naming it 3-SAT). 

Eventually (likely around step 5 or 6), DeepSeek will attempt to propose a shortcut (a heuristic algorithm) or attempt a massive proof by contradiction. **The Critic will brutally shoot it down.** 

You will force DeepSeek to backtrack and try a new branch of the tree. By doing this, you are forcing the AI to map out the exact boundaries of human mathematical ignorance without letting it trigger its "I can't solve P vs NP" safety rails.
