**[REVISED FRAMEWORK]:**  
We model the Diplomatic Banquet Protocol as the **Hitting Set problem on a 3-uniform hypergraph** \( H = (V, E) \) with a global budget parameter \( K \). The question becomes: does there exist a hitting set \( M \subseteq V \) with \( |M| \le K \)? I adopt the **parameterized complexity and kernelization perspective** rooted in graph structure theory, specifically using **crown decompositions** and **core/crown reductions** for hypergraphs. The goal is to reduce the instance size (the number of delegates and demands) in polynomial time to an equivalent "kernel" whose size depends only on \( K \), thereby isolating the combinatorial core that respects the global budget.

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:**  
**Lemma 1 (Crown Reduction for 3-Uniform Hypergraphs).**  
Let \( H = (V, E) \) be a 3-uniform hypergraph and \( K \) a non-negative integer. There exists a polynomial-time algorithm that either correctly concludes that no hitting set of size \( \le K \) exists, or identifies a **crown decomposition**: a partition of a subset of vertices into a "crown" \( C \subseteq V \) and a "head" \( Hd \subseteq V \) with \( C \cap Hd = \emptyset \), a matching \( M_{crown} \subseteq E \) of size \( |C| \) where each edge in \( M_{crown} \) contains exactly one vertex of \( C \) and the remaining vertices lie entirely within \( Hd \), and such that \( |C| > |Hd| \). The reduction then removes \( C \) and decrements \( K \) by \( |Hd| \). The resulting instance \( (H', K') \) is equivalent to the original: \( H \) has a hitting set of size \( \le K \) if and only if \( H' \) has a hitting set of size \( \le K' \).

**[JUSTIFICATION]:**  
This lemma directly addresses the global budget \( K \) by explicitly reducing both the instance size and the budget in lockstep. The crown decomposition guarantees that any hitting set covering the edges in the crown matching must include at least \( |Hd| \) vertices from \( Hd \cup C \), and selecting the entire head \( Hd \) is always optimal for covering those edges because it dominates the crown vertices. The reduction is mathematically sound due to the **Hall-type condition** inherent in the crown structure: the set of vertices in the crown \( C \) is strictly larger than the set of vertices in the head \( Hd \) that are necessary to cover the matched edges. Since the budget \( K \) is decremented by exactly the number of vertices we are forced to take from the head, the equivalence holds globally. This approach survives the Critic's attack by: (1) honoring the global budget via parameter update, (2) using a rigorous structural decomposition that exists for any hypergraph (not assuming tree-like structure), and (3) operating entirely within the graph-theoretic/topological paradigm through matching theory and vertex covering generalizations.
