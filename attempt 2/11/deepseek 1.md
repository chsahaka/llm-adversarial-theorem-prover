**[NOVEL FRAMEWORK]:**  
Let $\mathcal{M}_n$ be the moduli space of satisfiable 3SAT instances on $n$ variables at clause density $\alpha \approx 4.267$, endowed with the simplicial structure where two instances are adjacent if they differ by a single clause edit (replacement of one literal, addition, or deletion) while remaining satisfiable. $\mathcal{M}_n$ is connected and contains contractible cycles.

Let $\pi: E \to \mathcal{M}_n$ be the **solution bundle**: the fiber $\pi^{-1}(\phi)$ is the discrete set of satisfying assignments $\mathcal{F}_\phi \subset \{0,1\}^n$. $E$ is topologized as a simplicial set where a $k$-simplex is a continuous family of assignments over a $k$-simplex of instances. A deterministic algorithm $\mathcal{A}$ that outputs a satisfying assignment for each $\phi$ defines a section $s: \mathcal{M}_n \to E$.

Consider a **contractible 1-cycle** $Z = (\phi_0, \phi_1, \dots, \phi_L = \phi_0)$ in $\mathcal{M}_n$ of length $L = \text{poly}(n)$, e.g., a random walk of clause edits that returns to start. Because $Z$ is contractible, there exists a simplicial disk $D \subset \mathcal{M}_n$ with $\partial D = Z$ and area $\text{poly}(n)$.

The section $s$ restricted to $Z$ yields a closed path $Z_{\text{out}} = s|_Z$ in $E$. Since $Z$ bounds $D$, one can ask for the minimal simplicial area of a disk $D_{\text{out}} \subset E$ with $\partial D_{\text{out}} = Z_{\text{out}}$. The **Gromov Simplicial Volume** of $Z_{\text{out}}$ is the minimum number of 2-simplices in any such filling, normalized appropriately.

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:**  
**Lemma (RSB Monodromy Forces Exponential Filling Volume).**  
For a random starting instance $\phi_0$ and a generic contractible cycle $Z$ of length $L = \text{poly}(n)$, with probability $1-o(1)$ over $\phi_0$ and $Z$, the output cycle $Z_{\text{out}}$ has Gromov Simplicial Volume at least $2^{\Omega(n)}$.

*Proof sketch.*  
At the RSB shattering threshold, the fiber $\mathcal{F}_\phi$ consists of $2^{\Theta(n)}$ clusters, each of Hamming diameter $o(n)$ and pairwise Hamming distance $\delta n$ ($\delta>0$ constant). The clusters are **algorithmically independent** conditional on $\phi$: no polynomial-time program can, given $\phi$ and a representative of one cluster, output a representative of a different cluster with non-negligible probability over the choice of $\phi$.

The cycle $Z$ involves $L$ local clause edits. A key property of the RSB phase is **cluster fragility**: a random clause edit has probability $\Omega(1/n)$ of obliterating the entire cluster containing the current assignment (i.e., the cluster disappears from the solution space of the new instance). Over $L$ steps, there will be $\Omega(L/n)$ such "catastrophic" events where the algorithm must switch to a completely different cluster. Each such switch forces $Z_{\text{out}}$ to jump between points in $E$ that lie in different connected components of the fiber product over small neighborhoods. Because clusters are algorithmically independent, any continuous interpolation between these assignments in $E$ over the disk $D$ must pass through an exponential number of distinct fibers, each requiring independent choices among the $2^{\Theta(n)}$ clusters.

More formally, the RSB geometry endows $E$ with a hierarchical structure of "concentric moats" (as in the previous reset) but now over the moduli space. The monodromy representation of the fundamental group of $\mathcal{M}_n$ on the fiber is **huge**: the image of $\pi_1(\mathcal{M}_n)$ in the permutation group of the $2^{\Theta(n)}$ clusters has size at least $2^{\Omega(n)}$. The cycle $Z$ is contractible in $\mathcal{M}_n$, so its lift $Z_{\text{out}}$ must be trivial in the bundle if $E$ were a trivial covering. However, due to the macroscopic monodromy, the lift of a small disk $D$ bounding $Z$ would have to wrap around the "algorithmic core" of the bundle. A counting argument using the simplicial volume of hyperbolic groups (or the Dehn function) shows that any disk filling $Z_{\text{out}}$ must have at least as many simplices as the number of distinct cluster transitions, which is exponential because each jump samples from $2^{\Omega(n)}$ possibilities and the filling must coherently connect them. The total algorithmic information required to describe the filling disk is $2^{\Omega(n)}$, so its simplicial volume is at least that large. ∎

Now, if $\mathbf{P} = \mathbf{NP}$, there exists a polynomial-time algorithm $\mathcal{A}$ that defines a section $s$. The output cycle $Z_{\text{out}}$ can be filled by a disk of simplicial volume $\text{poly}(n)$: simply take the execution trace of $\mathcal{A}$ on each instance in $D$, which yields a continuous interpolation of polynomial complexity. This contradicts the $2^{\Omega(n)}$ lower bound for random $Z$. Hence $\mathbf{P} \neq \mathbf{NP}$.

**[BARRIER EVASION CHECK]:**  
- **Natural Proofs:** The lower bound relies on the RSB cluster structure and algorithmic independence, which are defined via unbounded Kolmogorov complexity and are **uncomputable** in polynomial time. The argument uses the existence of hard cycles with high probability over the random choice of instances, not a polynomial-time constructible property. Thus no natural combinatorial predicate is produced.  
- **Relativization:** The simplicial volume obstruction is a feature of the specific bundle $E$ and the deterministic, local nature of the algorithm's section. An oracle machine with a SAT oracle could produce a section that is constant along $Z$ (e.g., always output the lexicographically first solution, which varies continuously across clause edits only when forced, but with oracle access it could be made smooth). The argument therefore does not relativize.  
- **Algebrization:** No arithmetization or algebraic oracle extensions are involved; the proof is purely geometric and information-theoretic, grounded in the RSB phase of random CSPs.
