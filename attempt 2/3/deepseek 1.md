**[NOVEL FRAMEWORK]:**  
For a 3SAT instance $\phi$ on $n$ variables, let $\mathcal{C}_\phi$ denote the simplicial complex whose vertices are partial assignments (i.e., strings $v \in \{0,1,\ast\}^n$) that do not falsify any clause of $\phi$, and whose $k$-simplices are chains of $k+1$ partial assignments ordered by extension ($v \prec w$ if $w$ is obtained from $v$ by assigning values to $\ast$-variables). This is a subcomplex of the $n$-dimensional hypercube.  

Define the **information cocycle** $\omega_\phi \in Z^1(\mathcal{C}_\phi; \mathbb{Z})$ as follows. For each edge $e = (u,v)$ with $u \prec v$ and $|v| = |u|+1$, set $\omega_\phi(e) = |K(v \mid \phi) - K(u \mid \phi)|$, where $K(\cdot \mid \phi)$ is the **unbounded prefix-free Kolmogorov complexity** conditioned on the full description of $\phi$. Since $\omega_\phi$ is the coboundary of the $0$-cochain $v \mapsto K(v \mid \phi)$, it is indeed a $1$-cocycle.  
The **support** of $\omega_\phi$ is the set of edges $e$ with $\omega_\phi(e) > 0$. This cocycle measures the information-theoretic "jumps" required to move through the space of partial assignments.

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:**  
**Lemma (Cohomological Cut at the Phase Transition).**  
Fix a clause-to-variable ratio $\alpha \in (4.2, 4.3)$ and let $\phi$ be drawn uniformly from satisfiable 3SAT instances on $n$ variables with $\alpha n$ clauses. Then with probability $1-o(1)$ (as $n \to \infty$), for **every** path $\gamma = (v_0 = \emptyset, v_1, \dots, v_n = \sigma)$ in $\mathcal{C}_\phi$ where each $v_i$ is an $i$-variable partial assignment and $\sigma$ is a satisfying assignment, there exists at least one edge $e \in \gamma$ such that  
$$ \omega_\phi(e) \geq \epsilon n $$  
for some absolute constant $\epsilon > 0$. Equivalently, there is a partial assignment $v$ on the path with $K(v \mid \phi) \geq \epsilon n$.  

*Proof sketch.* The set of satisfying assignments of a random $\phi$ at the critical density is known to be shattered into exponentially many clusters that are pairwise isolated by linear Hamming distance and possess high algorithmic incompressibility conditional on $\phi$ (a consequence of the overlap structure and the Shannon complexity of the solution space). Any path from the empty assignment to a satisfying assignment must cross the "boundary" of the cluster containing $\sigma$, which occurs at a Hamming distance $\Theta(n)$ from $\emptyset$. At that boundary, the partial assignment $v$ must encode enough information about $\sigma$ to commit to the specific cluster, but since clusters are nearly orthogonal and $\phi$ alone does not identify any particular cluster, $K(v \mid \phi) \geq \delta n$ for some constant $\delta$. The cocycle $\omega_\phi$ being the discrete gradient of $K$ forces a jump of size $\Omega(n)$ along the crossing edge. ∎

**[BARRIER EVASION CHECK]:**  
- **Natural Proofs:** The invariant $K(v \mid \phi)$ is **uncomputable** even given $\phi$, and the support of $\omega_\phi$ cannot be computed or approximated in polynomial time. The argument does not produce a polynomial-time recognizable property that distinguishes a large fraction of functions; it relies on the non-constructive, information-theoretic structure of random satisfiable instances at the phase transition.  
- **Algebrization:** No arithmetization, low-degree extensions, or algebraic oracle separations are used. The cohomological cocycle is defined purely topologically, and the analysis rests on algorithmic information theory (Kolmogorov complexity), which does not algebrize. The result is relative to the unrelativized world and does not claim to hold for all oracles.
