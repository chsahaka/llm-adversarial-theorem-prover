**[NOVEL FRAMEWORK]:** We define the **Cohomological Configuration Space of Partial Assignments**, denoted $\mathcal{C}_n$ for a given 3SAT instance $\phi$ on $n$ variables. A point in $\mathcal{C}_n$ is a pair $(S, \sigma)$, where $S \subset \{1, \dots, n\}$ is a subset of instantiated variables and $\sigma: S \to \{0,1\}$ is an assignment. We topologize this set not via standard Hamming distance, but via the **Word Metric on the Non-Abelian Fundamental Groupoid**. Specifically, we construct a 2-dimensional CW-complex $X_\phi$ whose 1-skeleton is a Cayley graph of the free group $F_n$ (representing variable toggling operations as non-commutative generators $g_1, \dots, g_n$). We attach 2-cells corresponding to "clause satisfaction obstructions." A computation sequence corresponds to an edge-path. The NP-Complete "hole" is proposed to be a **persistent torsion element in the Second Homotopy Group** $\pi_2(X_\phi)$ that is nontrivial exactly when $\phi$ is satisfiable, yet any path representing a polynomial-time resolution must contract to a point in the associated **Nilpotent Quotient Space** $\mathcal{N}_k$ (where $k = O(\log n)$).

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:** We assert that the fundamental group $\pi_1(X_\phi)$ contains a subgroup isomorphic to the **Baumslag-Solitar Group $BS(1,2)$** precisely when the instance $\phi$ encodes a "self-referential reduction structure" (e.g., the global constraints of a typical NP-complete reduction). Let $\alpha$ be a loop in $X_\phi$ corresponding to the sequence of deterministic steps a polynomial-time algorithm takes to attempt to resolve a "spine" of the formula. We deduce that the **Dehn function** of the path-connected component containing the empty assignment is **non-polynomial**. Because polynomial-time computation corresponds to paths whose length bounds the area of their minimal van Kampen diagram, the existence of this $BS(1,2)$ distortion implies that traversing the space to the "Satisfying Fiber" (a set of points in $\mathcal{C}_n$ disconnected by 2-cells) requires a path whose **filling area grows exponentially relative to its length**. Thus, a polynomial-time sequence of operations (a short path) cannot possibly reach the acceptance region without intersecting the 2-skeleton in a way that requires super-polynomial time to verify (or "untangle").

**[BARRIER EVASION CHECK]:** This step escapes **Relativization** because the obstruction is rooted in the **Word Problem for Non-Abelian Groups** (specifically, the unsolvability of the isomorphism problem and the super-polynomial Dehn function of $BS(1,2)$). These are purely syntactic/combinatorial group theory properties that are invariant under the addition of oracles; an oracle for SAT does not collapse the metric distortion of the underlying Cayley graph. It escapes **Natural Proofs** because the "separating property" is not a combinatorial property of a Boolean function's truth table (which would be subject to the Natural Proofs largeness condition), but rather a **geometric invariant** (the second homotopy group torsion) of a space constructed *from the instance*, not from the function. The property of having $BS(1,2)$ distortion in the path groupoid is a topological invariant of the computation *trajectory*, not a property of the circuit family, and is thus not "naturalizable" in the sense of Razborov-Rudich.
