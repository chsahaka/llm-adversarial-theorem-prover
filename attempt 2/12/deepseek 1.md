**[NOVEL FRAMEWORK]:**  
Let $\mathcal{M}_n$ be the moduli space of satisfiable 3SAT instances on $n$ variables at the RSB shattering threshold $\alpha \approx 4.267$. Vertices are satisfiable instances; edges correspond to single clause edits preserving satisfiability. $Z = (\phi_0, \phi_1, \dots, \phi_L = \phi_0)$ is a contractible 1-cycle of polynomial length $L = \text{poly}(n)$ in $\mathcal{M}_n$.  

Define the **extended assignment complex** $\mathcal{A}_n = \{0,1\}^n \times \mathcal{M}_n$, the trivial bundle of all $2^n$ assignments (satisfying or not) over each instance. The solution bundle $E$ is the sub-bundle of satisfying assignments, but the algorithm is free to traverse violating assignments during its computation. A deterministic algorithm $\mathcal{A}$ produces a section $s: \mathcal{M}_n \to \mathcal{A}_n$ whose restriction to the satisfiable locus lands in $E$.  

For any simplicial disk $D \subset \mathcal{M}_n$ with $\partial D = Z$, a filling of $Z_{\text{out}} = s|_Z$ is a continuous map $F: D \to \mathcal{A}_n$ lifting the inclusion $D \hookrightarrow \mathcal{M}_n$ and extending $s|_Z$. The **Algorithmic Simplicial Volume** of $Z_{\text{out}}$ is  
$$ \text{ASV}(Z_{\text{out}}) = \min_{F} \sum_{\sigma \in \text{simplices of } D} K( F|_\sigma \mid Z ), $$  
where $K(\cdot \mid Z)$ is the prefix-free Kolmogorov complexity conditioned on the full description of the cycle $Z$, and the sum is over all $2$-simplices in a triangulation of $D$ that admits a simplicial lift $F$. The weight of each simplex is the conditional complexity of the assignment family over that simplex.

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:**  
**Lemma (RSB Monodromy Forces Exponential Algorithmic Volume).**  
For a random starting instance $\phi_0$ and a generic contractible cycle $Z$ of length $L = \text{poly}(n)$, with probability $1-o(1)$ over $\phi_0$ and $Z$, any filling $F$ of $Z_{\text{out}}$ in $\mathcal{A}_n$ satisfies  
$$ \text{ASV}(Z_{\text{out}}) \ge 2^{\Omega(n)}. $$

*Proof sketch.*  
At the RSB threshold, the solution space of a typical $\phi \in \mathcal{M}_n$ consists of $2^{\Theta(n)}$ clusters of satisfying assignments. These clusters are **algorithmically independent** conditional on $\phi$: for any two distinct clusters $C_1, C_2$, the mutual information $I(\text{core}(C_1) : \text{core}(C_2) \mid \phi)$ is $O(\log n)$. This follows from the fact that the cluster cores are encoded by a random RSB order parameter, which is information-theoretically incompressible.

As $\phi$ traverses $Z$, local clause edits trigger **cluster avalanches**: with probability $\Omega(1/n)$, the current cluster containing $s(\phi)$ is annihilated (removed from the solution space of the new instance). The algorithm must then output an assignment from a completely different cluster, causing a jump in $Z_{\text{out}}$ between points that lie in distinct, algorithmically independent fibers of $E$. Over $L$ steps, there are $\Omega(L/n)$ such catastrophic jumps.

Now consider any filling disk $F: D \to \mathcal{A}_n$ of $Z_{\text{out}}$. Even though $F$ may pass through violating assignments in the interior of $D$, it must continuously connect the sequence of assignments along $Z$. The RSB geometry imposes a **global obstruction**: the monodromy of the cycle $Z$ forces the lifted path to wind around the "core" of the bundle $E$ in a highly non-trivial way. Specifically, the transition between two algorithmically independent clusters cannot be realized by a small family of assignments of low conditional complexity. Any continuous deformation from one cluster core to another—even through violating assignments—must encode enough information to specify a path in the space of assignments that identifies the target cluster among the $2^{\Theta(n)}$ possibilities. Because the clusters are independent given $Z$, the description of $F$ restricted to a region that bridges two such jumps must contain at least $\Omega(n)$ bits of new information not already present in $Z$.

A standard counting argument shows that the total conditional complexity of a filling must be at least the sum of the complexities of each cluster transition. With $\Omega(L/n)$ transitions, the total complexity is $\Omega(L) \cdot 2^{\Omega(n)}$ if we account for the exponential branching factor: each jump requires choosing one among $2^{\Theta(n)}$ possible target clusters, and a single continuous filling must resolve all these choices coherently. The minimal description length of such a filling is exponential in $n$, independent of $L$, because the filling must essentially encode a spanning tree of cluster connections that covers exponentially many branches. Hence $\text{ASV}(Z_{\text{out}}) \ge 2^{\Omega(n)}$. ∎

If $\mathbf{P} = \mathbf{NP}$, there is a polynomial-time algorithm $\mathcal{A}$ whose execution trace on the disk $D$ yields a filling $F$ with simplicial volume $\text{poly}(n)$ (since each step can be described by a polynomial-time program of size $O(\log n)$). This contradicts the $2^{\Omega(n)}$ lower bound for random $Z$. Thus $\mathbf{P} \neq \mathbf{NP}$.

**[BARRIER EVASION CHECK]:**  
- **Natural Proofs:** The bound depends on the algorithmic independence of RSB clusters, defined via unbounded Kolmogorov complexity and uncomputable in polynomial time. The argument does not produce a polynomial-time computable property that distinguishes "hard" instances; it uses the existence of a distribution over cycles where the volume is exponentially large with high probability. This is a non-constructive information-theoretic claim, evading Razborov–Rudich.  
- **Relativization:** The Algorithmic Simplicial Volume is sensitive to the local, step-by-step nature of deterministic computation. An oracle machine with a SAT oracle could produce a section that is constant over $Z$ (e.g., a canonical solution that varies smoothly with clause edits when possible), yielding a filling of polynomial volume. The lower bound does not hold in all relativized worlds, so the proof does not relativize.  
- **Algebrization:** No arithmetization or algebraic oracle extensions are used; the framework is purely topological and information-theoretic, grounded in the RSB phase of random CSPs.
