   $$\|C_M(D)\|_1 \le A \cdot T \cdot S = \text{poly}(n) \cdot \text{poly}(n) \cdot \text{poly}(n) = \text{poly}(n)$$
4. **The Boundary Maps:** The topological boundary of the computation solid $C_M(D)$ consists of three components:
   $$\partial C_M(D) = D_{out} - D_{in} + C_M(Z)$$

   - $D_{out}$ is the disk of final output configurations at $t=T$.
5. **The Pushforward Filling:** The disk $D_{out}$ lives entirely in the solution bundle $E$ and its boundary is exactly $\partial D_{out} = Z_{out}$. Therefore, $D_{out}$ is a valid filling for $Z_{out}$.
   By homological bounds, the volume of this output disk is constrained by the volume of the computation that generated it:
   $$\|D_{out}\|_1 \le \|C_M(D)\|_1 + \|D_{in}\|_1 = \text{poly}(n) + \text{poly}(n) = \text{poly}(n)$$
