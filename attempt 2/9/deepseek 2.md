**[NOVEL FRAMEWORK]:**  
We retain the **Cook–Levin 2D CW-Complex** $T_M(\phi)$ for a deterministic Turing machine $M$ on input $\phi$ (a 3SAT instance). The 0‑cells are $(t,p)$ with $t\in\{0,\dots,T\}$ and $p\in\mathbb{Z}$. The 1‑cells consist of spatial edges $(t,p)\sim(t,p+1)$ and temporal edges $(t,p)\sim(t+1,p+\Delta)$ for $|\Delta|\le 1$. The 2‑cells are attached along the 3‑neighborhood loop $\{p-1,p,p+1\}$ to enforce the deterministic local update rule of $M$. The initial row $t=0$ encodes $\phi$, and the final row $t=T$ is the output.

Within this space, we define the **Worldline** $\Gamma_M$ as the 1‑dimensional subcomplex given by the exact trajectory of the read/write head: a path of 0‑cells $(t, p_t)$ connected by temporal 1‑cells $(t,p_t)\sim(t+1,p_{t+1})$. The rest of the tape is static memory updated only when $\Gamma_M$ passes through.

We project the computation onto **Alpha's Constraint Space** $\mathcal{C}_n(\phi)$ of partial assignments. There exists a continuous evaluation map  
$$\Phi: T_M(\phi) \longrightarrow \mathcal{C}_n(\phi)$$  
that sends each point in the tableau to the current partial assignment encoded on the tape at that time and position. The map $\Phi$ is **2‑Lipschitz** with respect to the natural graph metrics: a single step of the machine changes at most one tape cell (the one at the head), hence alters the assignment of at most one variable, moving the image point in $\mathcal{C}_n(\phi)$ by distance at most $2$ in the Hamming graph of partial assignments.

Alpha has constructed a nontrivial cohomology class $\alpha \in H^1(\mathcal{C}_n(\phi); \mathbb{Z})$ whose evaluation on loops measures a **Topological Winding Number** around the essential obstructions in the constraint space. For satisfiable expander instances $\phi$, the class $\alpha$ is nontrivial and its pullback $\Phi^*(\alpha)$ defines a winding number $W(\Gamma_M)$ for the worldline together with its induced updates.

**[PROPOSED STEP 5]:**  
We establish a strict **Winding Rate Bound**. Consider the projection of $\Gamma_M$ into $\mathcal{C}_n(\phi)$ under $\Phi$. Because the 2‑cells enforce strictly local $3\times 1$ updates, the map $\Phi$ is **Lipschitz in the spatial direction**: to change the assignment of two variables that are far apart on the tape, the worldline must physically travel between their encodings. A **macroscopic cycle** in $\mathcal{C}_n(\phi)$—one that encloses a nontrivial homology class and thus contributes $+1$ to the winding number—corresponds to a sequence of variable assignments that collectively traverse a generator of $H_1(\mathcal{C}_n(\phi))$. Due to the expander property of $\phi$, any such generator involves at least $\Omega(n)$ distinct variables whose encodings are distributed across the tape. The worldline, moving at speed $1$ cell per step, can visit at most $O(T)$ tape cells in $T$ steps. Therefore, the number of complete macroscopic cycles it can traverse satisfies  
$$W(\Gamma_M) \le O\!\left(\frac{T}{\Omega(n)}\right).$$  
In particular, each full **sweep** of the tape (a traversal from one end to the other) can increase the winding number by at most $O(1)$, because a single sweep can at best encompass one independent cycle in the cohomology of the constraint space.

**Alpha's Aggregate Bound** asserts that for a satisfiable 3SAT instance drawn from the expander ensemble, any continuous path in $\mathcal{C}_n(\phi)$ that starts at the empty assignment and ends at a satisfying total assignment must have winding number at least $2^{\delta n}$ for some constant $\delta>0$. This is a consequence of the exponential size of the monodromy group acting on the fiber of partial assignments. Equating the two bounds yields  
$$2^{\delta n} \le W(\Gamma_M) \le O\!\left(\frac{T}{n}\right) \quad\Longrightarrow\quad T \ge \Omega(n \cdot 2^{\delta n}) = 2^{\Omega(n)}.$$  
Thus any deterministic Turing machine that solves all such instances requires exponential time. Since the instances are satisfiable, we conclude $\mathbf{P} \neq \mathbf{NP}$.

**[BARRIER EVASION CHECK]:**  
- **Relativization (Baker–Gill–Solovay):** An oracle Turing machine $M^O$ augments the 2D tableau with **topological wormholes**—hyperedges of the form $(t,p) \sim (t+1,q)$ for arbitrary $p,q$, representing instantaneous oracle calls. These hyperedges allow the worldline to **teleport** across the spatial tape, circumventing the Lipschitz spatial bound. In one step, the head can jump from one side of a macroscopic cut to the other, traversing an entire homology cycle and incrementing the winding number by an arbitrary amount. The Winding Rate Bound collapses, and the same argument would yield a polynomial upper bound for any oracle that makes $\mathbf{P}^O = \mathbf{NP}^O$. Since our lower bound depends essentially on the *unrelativized* local geometry of the 2‑cells (which is destroyed by wormholes), the proof is **non‑relativizing**.  
- **Natural Proofs:** The set of hard instances is defined by the expander property of the incidence graph, a feature of the *syntactic representation* rather than the Boolean function computed (which for satisfiable instances is trivial). This set is **sparse** (random instances at critical density) and **not polynomial‑time recognizable** (deciding expansion is coNP‑hard). Hence the argument evades the largeness and constructivity criteria of Razborov–Rudich.
