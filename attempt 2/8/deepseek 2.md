**[NOVEL FRAMEWORK]:**  
We retain the **Cook–Levin 2D CW-Complex** $T_M(\phi)$ as the computational space for a deterministic Turing machine $M$ on input $\phi$ (a 3SAT instance). The 0‑cells are $(t,p)$ with $t \in \{0,\dots,T\}$ and $p \in \mathbb{Z}$; 1‑cells are spatial $(t,p)\sim(t,p+1)$ and temporal $(t,p)\sim(t+1,p+\Delta)$ with $|\Delta|\le 1$; and 2‑cells enforce the deterministic local update rule over the 3‑neighborhood $\{p-1,p,p+1\}$.  

Within this space, we isolate the **Worldline** $\Gamma_M \subset T_M(\phi)$ as the 1‑dimensional subcomplex consisting of the exact sequence of tape positions visited by the read/write head over time. Formally, $\Gamma_M$ is a path of 0‑cells $(t, p_t)$ where $p_t$ is the head position at step $t$, connected by temporal 1‑cells $(t, p_t) \sim (t+1, p_{t+1})$. This worldline is the *active* locus of computation; the rest of the tape is static memory updated only when the worldline passes through.

We now map the space of global configurations to a **cyclic covering space** that captures the monodromy of the 3SAT constraints. Specifically, for an expander instance $\phi$, there exists a nontrivial cohomology class $\alpha \in H^1(\mathcal{C}_n(\phi); \mathbb{Z})$ (the "Satisfiability Obstruction") whose evaluation on loops in assignment space yields a winding number. The worldline $\Gamma_M$, together with the tape content it modifies, defines a continuous map  
$$ \Phi: T_M(\phi) \to \mathcal{C}_n(\phi) $$  
from the tableau to the assignment configuration space. The **Winding Number** $W(\Gamma_M)$ is the pullback $\Phi^*(\alpha)$ evaluated on a canonical 1‑cycle that wraps around the "time" direction of the tableau. Intuitively, $W(\Gamma_M)$ counts how many times the machine's cumulative updates have cycled through independent nontrivial loops in the constraint satisfaction space.

**[PROPOSED STEP 5]:**  
We establish a **Winding Rate Bound** for any deterministic Turing machine subject to the local 3‑neighborhood 2‑cells. Because each 2‑cell enforces that a tape symbol change at $(t,p)$ depends only on the symbols in $\{p-1,p,p+1\}$ at time $t-1$ and the head state, the worldline can affect the winding number only when it physically traverses a **topological defect**—a spatial region where the assignment space has a nontrivial holonomy.  

The key geometric observation is that the local update rule makes the map $\Phi$ **1‑Lipschitz** in the following sense: a single time step can alter the assignment of at most one variable (the one whose encoding lies at the current head position). Consequently, to increment the winding number $W$ by a full unit, the worldline must perform a complete **sweep** across a macroscopic cut of the instance—a traversal that visits a set of tape cells whose variables span an independent cycle in the cohomology of $\mathcal{C}_n(\phi)$. Due to the expander property, any such macroscopic cycle has **linear length** $\Omega(n)$ in the number of variables, and the head can only advance one cell per step. Hence:  
$$ \Delta W \le \frac{\text{number of steps in sweep}}{\Omega(n)} \le \frac{T}{\Omega(n)}. $$  
In particular, the winding number after $T$ steps satisfies $W(\Gamma_M) \le O(T/n)$.

Now, **Alpha's Aggregate Bound** shows that for a satisfiable 3SAT instance drawn from the expander ensemble, any valid sequence of local updates that reaches a satisfying assignment must accumulate a total winding number of at least $2^{\delta n}$ (for some $\delta>0$). This is because the space of partial assignments has an **exponential monodromy representation**—a fiber bundle over a circle with structure group of size $2^{\Theta(n)}$—and resolving all constraints forces the worldline to wind around this circle that many times.  

Equating the two bounds yields:  
$$ 2^{\delta n} \le W(\Gamma_M) \le O\!\left(\frac{T}{n}\right) \quad\Longrightarrow\quad T \ge \Omega(n \cdot 2^{\delta n}) = 2^{\Omega(n)}. $$  
Thus any deterministic Turing machine that correctly decides all such instances must consume exponential time. Since the instances are satisfiable, this separates $\mathbf{P}$ from $\mathbf{NP}$.

**[BARRIER EVASION CHECK]:**  
- **Relativization (Baker–Gill–Solovay):** An oracle machine $M^O$ possesses, in addition to the local 2‑cells, **topological wormholes**—hyperedges of the form $(t,p) \sim (t+1,q)$ for arbitrary $p,q$, representing instantaneous oracle queries. These hyperedges allow the worldline to **teleport** across the spatial tape, circumventing the linear sweep requirement. A single oracle call can jump the head from one side of a macroscopic cut to the other, incrementing the winding number by an arbitrary amount in $O(1)$ steps. The Winding Rate Bound collapses entirely, and the same argument would falsely prove $\mathbf{P}^O = \mathbf{NP}^O$ for any oracle $O$. Since our lower bound hinges on the *unrelativized* local geometry of the 2D tableau—a geometry destroyed by wormholes—the proof is **non‑relativizing**.  
- **Natural Proofs:** The set of hard instances is defined by the expander property of the incidence graph, a property of the *syntactic encoding* rather than the Boolean function computed. Moreover, this set is **sparse** (random instances at critical density) and **not polynomial‑time recognizable** (deciding expansion is coNP‑hard). Hence the argument evades the largeness and constructivity criteria of Razborov–Rudich.
