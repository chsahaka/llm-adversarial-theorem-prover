**[NOVEL FRAMEWORK]:**  
We model the deterministic execution of a Turing machine $M$ on a 3SAT instance $\phi$ as a **strictly local Cellular Automaton (CA)** evolving on the 2D Cook–Levin Tableau $T_M(\phi)$. The state space at each cell $(t,p)$ is the product of the tape alphabet $\Sigma$ and the head presence/state $Q$. The update rule is given by a local transition function $F$ that depends only on the 3‑neighborhood $\{p-1,p,p+1\}$ at the previous time step, together with the current head state. Thus, the global configuration $\mathbf{c}_t \in \Sigma^S \times Q$ evolves as $\mathbf{c}_{t+1} = \mathcal{F}(\mathbf{c}_t)$, where $\mathcal{F}$ is a **strictly local, deterministic, discrete-time dynamical system**.

We embed this dynamics into **Alpha's Continuous Solution Landscape** $\mathcal{L}(\phi)$, a high‑dimensional energy functional over the space of partial assignments, derived from the constraint sheaf and the monodromy obstruction. The empty assignment corresponds to a point in a **false minimum** region, while satisfying assignments lie in **global minima** at energy zero. The Overlap Gap Property (OGP), rigorously established for random expander instances, asserts that the configuration space is partitioned into exponentially many well‑separated clusters of local minima, and that **any continuous path from the empty assignment to a satisfying assignment must cross an extensive region where the overlap with all low‑energy states is bounded away from both 0 and 1**. In this intermediate region, the **local gradient** (the direction of steepest descent in energy) is exponentially small or points in contradictory directions due to frustration. Consequently, no global guiding gradient exists.

**[PROPOSED STEP 6]:**  
We prove the **Algorithmic Sub‑Diffusion Bound**. Because the CA update rule $\mathcal{F}$ is strictly local, the trajectory of the configuration $\mathbf{c}_t$ in the assignment landscape $\mathcal{L}(\phi)$ is constrained by the **Lipschitz continuity** of the evaluation map: a single time step changes at most one variable's assignment, moving the image point by Hamming distance at most $1$. In the continuous relaxation, this corresponds to a bounded speed in the landscape. More importantly, the local nature of $\mathcal{F}$ implies that the system can only **follow local gradients**; it cannot sense or directly move toward distant basins. When the trajectory enters the OGP forbidden region, the local gradient signal vanishes (or becomes dominated by noise from local constraint frustration). A deterministic CA, lacking non‑local information, is then forced to perform an **exhaustive local search**—essentially a random walk on the energy landscape, but deterministically covering a local neighborhood. By standard results on the **mixing time of local Markov chains** in OGP landscapes, any local process requires time exponential in the barrier width to escape a metastable region. Concretely, the overlap gap width is $\Omega(n)$, and the system must traverse a Hamming distance of $\Omega(n)$ in a space with no consistent gradient. The number of locally available moves is polynomial, leading to a **Poincaré recurrence time** or exit time that scales as $2^{\Omega(n)}$.

Thus, to cross the OGP gap and reach a global minimum, the CA must simulate an exponential number of local updates. Therefore, the number of time steps satisfies $T \ge 2^{\Omega(n)}$. Since the TM is a special case of such a CA, any deterministic polynomial‑time Turing machine will fail to find a satisfying assignment for these hard expander instances, proving $\mathbf{P} \neq \mathbf{NP}$.

**[BARRIER EVASION CHECK]:**  
- **Relativization (Baker–Gill–Solovay):** An oracle Turing machine $M^O$ introduces **non‑local hyper‑edges** that act as **Global Gradient Sensors**. A single oracle query can compute the projection of the global state onto the solution space, effectively providing an instantaneous macroscopic jump across the OGP forbidden region. In the landscape picture, the oracle adds "wormholes" that directly connect the initial region to a global minimum, bypassing the local gradient constraint. The Algorithmic Sub‑Diffusion Bound collapses, and the same argument would yield a polynomial‑time traversal in a relativized world. Since our lower bound relies on the strictly local, gradient‑following nature of the unrelativized CA, the proof is **non‑relativizing**.  
- **Natural Proofs:** The hard instances are defined by the Overlap Gap Property, which is a geometric feature of the specific syntactic instance (expander graph) and not a property of the Boolean function computed (which for satisfiable instances is trivially constant). The set of such instances is sparse and not polynomial‑time recognizable. Hence the argument evades the largeness and constructivity criteria of Razborov–Rudich.
