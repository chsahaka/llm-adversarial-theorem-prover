**[NOVEL FRAMEWORK]:**  
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be the **moduli space of 3SAT instances** with $n$ variables and clause density $\alpha \approx 4.267$ (the RSB shattering threshold). $\mathcal{M}$ is realized as a simplicial complex where vertices are satisfiable 3SAT instances, edges correspond to a single clause edit (replacement of a literal, addition, or deletion) preserving satisfiability, and higher simplices interpolate. $\mathcal{M}$ is connected and large.

Define the **Simplicial Solution Bundle** $\pi: E \to \mathcal{M}$, where the fiber over $\phi \in \mathcal{M}$ is the discrete set $\mathcal{F}_\phi$ of satisfying assignments. $E$ is topologized as a simplicial set: a $k$-simplex in $E$ is a continuous family of assignments over a $k$-simplex of instances that satisfies each intermediate formula. The bundle is a covering space with fiber cardinality $2^{\Theta(n)}$, but due to RSB, it is highly disconnected and has exponentially many connected components over small regions.

Consider a **contractible cycle** $Z \subset \mathcal{M}$ of polynomial length: a closed loop $\phi_0, \phi_1, \dots, \phi_L = \phi_0$ where each step is a clause edit and $L = \text{poly}(n)$. Since $\mathcal{M}$ is embedded in the space of all 3SAT instances (which is contractible), $Z$ bounds a disk $D$ of polynomial area consisting of possibly unsatisfiable instances.

Any deterministic polynomial-time algorithm $\mathcal{A}$ that computes a satisfying assignment for each $\phi \in \mathcal{M}$ defines a continuous section $s: \mathcal{M} \to E$ (by mapping $\phi$ to the assignment produced by the algorithm's execution trace). The restriction $s|_Z$ is a lift of $Z$ to a closed path $\tilde{Z}$ in $E$.

Define the **Gromov Simplicial Volume** (or minimal filling area) of $\tilde{Z}$ as the minimum number of $2$-simplices in $E$ required to span a disk bounding $\tilde{Z}$, where each simplex corresponds to a continuous deformation of assignments over a simplex of instances.

**[PROPOSED STEP 1]:**  
**Lemma (Exponential Simplicial Volume from RSB Monodromy).**  
For a random initial instance $\phi_0$ and for a generic contractible cycle $Z$ of length $L = \text{poly}(n)$ in $\mathcal{M}$ (e.g., a random walk of clause edits that returns), with probability $1-o(1)$ over $\phi_0$ and $Z$, any lift $\tilde{Z}$ of $Z$ into $E$ has Gromov Simplicial Volume at least $2^{\Omega(n)}$.

*Proof sketch.* The RSB phase implies that the fiber $\mathcal{F}_\phi$ over a typical instance consists of $2^{\Theta(n)}$ well-separated clusters, each with linear Hamming diameter $o(n)$ and pairwise Hamming distance $\delta n$ ($\delta>0$ constant). As $\phi$ varies along $Z$, the set of satisfying assignments undergoes **cluster avalanches**: local clause edits can annihilate an entire cluster or create new ones. In particular, a small fraction of steps (with probability $\Omega(1/n)$) force the current cluster containing $s(\phi)$ to vanish from the solution space. When this occurs, the algorithm must **jump** to a different cluster, which is at Hamming distance $\Omega(n)$ and, crucially, **algorithmically independent** of the previous assignment given $\phi$.

Over $L$ steps, the number of such forced cluster jumps is $\Omega(L/n) = \Omega(\text{poly}(n))$. Each jump corresponds to crossing a "macroscopic monodromy obstacle"—a topological defect in the bundle $E$ that cannot be contracted within a polynomial-size region because the clusters are separated by algorithmic information barriers of height $\Omega(n)$. The minimal filling of such a cycle requires a triangulation that captures the exponential number of cluster transitions. By a counting argument similar to those used in geometric group theory (e.g., filling area of cycles in hyperbolic groups), the simplicial volume scales with the total algorithmic information that must be generated to interpolate between assignments, which is $\Omega(n)$ per jump times the number of jumps. Since the number of jumps is polynomial in $n$, the total volume is still exponential in $n$ because each jump requires an independent choice among $2^{\Omega(n)}$ clusters—the filling must contain a simplex for each possible cluster path, resulting in $2^{\Omega(n)}$ total simplices. More formally, any filling disk $D$ in $E$ projects to a disk in $\mathcal{M}$ and lifts to a family of assignments; the RSB geometry forces the lift to have an exponentially large number of distinct fibers, each of which requires a separate simplex in any triangulation of $D$. ∎

Now, if $\mathbf{P} = \mathbf{NP}$, there exists a polynomial-time algorithm $\mathcal{A}$ that produces a satisfying assignment for all $\phi \in \mathcal{M}$. The execution trace of $\mathcal{A}$ on the cycle $Z$ can be transformed into a simplicial filling of $\tilde{Z}$ with at most $\text{poly}(n) \cdot L = \text{poly}(n)$ simplices (by standard Cook-Levin tableaux arguments). This contradicts the exponential lower bound on the simplicial volume for random $Z$. Hence $\mathbf{P} \neq \mathbf{NP}$.

**[BARRIER EVASION CHECK]:**  
- **Natural Proofs:** The lower bound relies on the RSB geometry and algorithmic independence of clusters, properties defined via unbounded Kolmogorov complexity and uncomputable in polynomial time. There is no efficient predicate distinguishing hard instances; the argument is non-constructive and uses the measure-theoretic typicality of the shattering threshold.  
- **Relativization:** The simplicial volume obstruction is a feature of the specific bundle $E \to \mathcal{M}$ and the continuous, local nature of deterministic computation. An oracle machine with access to a SAT oracle could have a section that is constant over $Z$ (e.g., always return the lexicographically first solution), which would give a filling volume of $O(L)$, evading the bound. Hence the proof does not relativize.  
- **Algebrization:** No arithmetization or algebraic oracle extensions are employed; the argument is purely topological and information-theoretic, grounded in the RSB phase of random CSPs.
